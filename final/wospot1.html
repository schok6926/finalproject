Spotlight 1: Marie Curie

Marie Sklodowska Curie was born in Warsaw, Poland, on November 7, 1867. Unable to attend university in her native Poland, she moved to France to attend the the Sarbonne. Curie would go on to win two Noble Prizes, one for Physics in 1903, and another for Chemistry in 1911. She was the first woman to ever win the Nobel Prize, and became the first person, man or woman, to win the prestigous award twice. However, she is famed for discovering the elements Polonium and Radium.

Spotlight 2: Najat Vallaud-Belkacem

Born on October 4, 1977, in a rural village in the Moroccan countryside of Bni Chiker, Najat Vallaud-Belkacem immigrated with her mother and older sister to France when she was four years old. She went on to become Minister of Women's Affairs, Minister of City Affairs, Minister of Youth Affairs and Sports, and Government spokesperson. She would also become the first French woman to be appointed Minister of Education, Higher Education, and Research.

Spotlight 3: Zhang Xin

Now a billionare businesswoman, Zhang Xin used to work as a factory worker in Hong Kong for five years until she earned enough to support an education abroad. She co-founded the company SOHO China, which she is now the CEO of, with her husband Pan Shiyi.

Spotlight 4: Amal Alamuddin Clooney

Born in Beirut, Lebanon, Amal Clooney is a Lebanese-British barrister(a lawyer) at Doughty Street Chambers, specializing in international law and human rights. Her family immigrated to Buckinghamshire England, fleeing from the Lebanese Civil War, when she was two. In 2019, she was appointed the special envoy of media freedom by the British Foreign and Commonwealth Office.
